## Section 2 — Oracle Fusion Talent Management Suite Overview

### 2.1 Suite Positioning

Oracle Fusion Talent Management is a suite of integrated modules within Oracle Fusion Human Capital Management, designed to enable organisations to identify, develop, measure, and retain talent systematically. APPSolve implements the Talent Management suite as a cohesive capability — not as isolated modules — ensuring that talent data, performance outcomes, succession decisions, and development plans are connected and mutually reinforcing.

The modules within the Oracle Fusion Talent Management suite that APPSolve implements include:

| Module | Core purpose |
|---|---|
| **Talent Profiles** | Defines the skills, competencies, qualifications, and experience expected for each role and recorded for each worker |
| **Goal Management** | Enables corporate-to-individual goal cascading, SMART goal setting, and ongoing progress tracking |
| **Performance Management** | Structures performance review cycles — mid-year, year-end, and ongoing feedback |
| **Talent Review** | Provides manager and HR visibility of talent population across performance and potential dimensions |
| **Career Development** | Supports employee career planning, development objectives, and internal mobility |
| **Succession Planning** | Identifies successors for key roles and tracks readiness and development of the succession pipeline |

### 2.2 Platform Foundation

The Oracle Fusion Talent Management suite operates on the same cloud-native SaaS infrastructure as Oracle HCM Core — receiving automatic quarterly feature releases, benefiting from Oracle's ongoing AI and platform investment, and operating within the same security, privacy, and audit framework. APPSolve manages talent module configuration and quarterly release assessment alongside the client's broader Oracle HCM estate.

### 2.3 AI-Assisted Talent Capabilities

Oracle Fusion Talent Management incorporates AI-assisted features that APPSolve activates and configures as part of the talent module implementation:

- **AI skill recommendations** — Oracle's embedded AI analyses worker profiles, learning completions, and career goals to suggest relevant skill development priorities
- **Talent search and matching** — AI-powered search surfaces workers whose profiles most closely match a target role or opportunity
- **Performance calibration support** — AI-assisted tools flag potential rating bias patterns during calibration sessions, supporting more objective talent assessments

*Note: AI feature availability and specific capability is subject to Oracle's quarterly release schedule and the client's Oracle SaaS subscription tier. APPSolve advises clients on feature activation timing as part of release management services. All AI capabilities referenced here are Oracle Fusion HCM platform capabilities; they do not represent APPSolve-developed AI functionality.*

### 2.4 HCM Core Dependency

All Oracle Fusion Talent Management modules depend on Oracle HCM Core being implemented first. The organisational model, job profiles, position hierarchy, and worker records created in the HCM Core phase are the foundation that Talent Management reads, extends, and enriches. APPSolve does not implement Talent Management as a standalone capability — it is always sequenced after, and dependent on, a stable and validated HCM Core environment.

## Section 3 — Talent Profiles and Job Profiles

### 3.1 The Talent Profile Foundation

Talent profiles are the architectural foundation of the Oracle Fusion Talent Management suite. Every talent process — performance reviews, goal setting, succession planning, career development — references and contributes to the talent profile. APPSolve treats talent profile design as the most consequential configuration decision in a Talent Management engagement: a well-designed profile structure enables all subsequent talent processes to function accurately; a poorly designed one creates rework that propagates across every module.

APPSolve's approach: talent profile design is completed, workshopped, and signed off before any other talent module configuration begins.

### 3.2 Person Profiles

APPSolve configures Oracle Fusion HCM person profiles to capture the skills, competencies, qualifications, certifications, experience, and accomplishments of each worker:

| Profile element | Description |
|---|---|
| Skills and competencies | Worker-declared and manager-validated skill and competency ratings against the client's competency framework |
| Qualifications and education | Degrees, diplomas, professional qualifications, and academic achievements |
| Certifications and licences | Professional registrations, trade certificates, safety certifications — with expiry tracking |
| Work experience | Internal and external experience history; key roles and responsibilities |
| Languages | Language proficiency ratings |
| Accomplishments | Awards, recognition, publications, project achievements |
| Risk and impact | Risk-of-loss and impact-of-loss indicators populated from talent review assessments |

Worker self-service enables employees to maintain and update their own profile data, with manager and HR visibility and validation controls. Profile completeness dashboards allow HR to track adoption and identify workers with incomplete talent records.

### 3.3 Job Profiles

APPSolve configures job profiles to define the required skills, competencies, qualifications, and experience for each role in the organisation. Job profiles serve as the benchmark against which worker profiles are compared — enabling skill gap analysis, internal talent matching, recruiting requirement definition, and succession eligibility assessment.

**Job profile components APPSolve configures:**

| Component | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Required competencies and proficiency levels | Defines the competency expectations for the role |
| Required qualifications | Educational and professional qualification requirements |
| Required certifications | Mandatory licences, registrations, or safety certifications |
| Preferred skills | Skills that are advantageous but not mandatory |
| Job family and career stream alignment | Links the job profile into the career pathway architecture |

Job profiles are configured collaboratively with the client's HR and talent leadership during the Talent Design Workshop phase of the implementation.

### 3.4 Profile Matching and Talent Search

With both person profiles and job profiles configured, Oracle Fusion HCM provides real-time talent search and matching:

- **Internal talent search** — HR and talent leaders can search for workers whose profiles match a target job profile or a specified set of skills
- **Gap analysis** — the system identifies the delta between a worker's current profile and the requirements of a target role, informing development plan priorities
- **Succession matching** — the succession planning module uses job profile requirements to assess readiness and suitability of succession candidates

## Section 4 — Goal Management

### 4.1 Goal Frameworks

APPSolve implements Oracle Fusion Goal Management to provide a structured, organisation-wide approach to goal setting, cascading, and tracking. The goal framework is designed in collaboration with the client's HR and business leadership to ensure it reflects the organisation's performance management philosophy and review calendar.

**Goal framework elements APPSolve configures:**

| Element | Configuration |
|---|---|
| Goal plan types | Corporate, BU, team, and individual goal plans — configured per review cycle |
| Goal libraries | Pre-approved goal templates that employees and managers select from, reducing effort and ensuring alignment |
| Goal cascading | Corporate strategic objectives cascade down to BU, team, and individual level |
| SMART goal structure | Configurable goal fields — specific, measurable, time-bound, with target dates and achievement tracking |
| Goal weights | Weighted goal plans where goals carry relative importance scores that feed the performance score calculation |
| Goal approval workflows | Manager or HR approval for goal additions, changes, and closures |

### 4.2 Progress Tracking and Check-ins

APPSolve configures Oracle Fusion Goal Management to support ongoing progress tracking throughout the review cycle, not only at formal review points:

- **Progress updates** — employees update goal progress status and percentage completion on a self-service basis
- **Manager visibility** — managers see real-time goal status for all direct reports from their manager dashboard
- **Check-in conversations** — structured check-in records can be logged against each goal, creating an ongoing coaching record
- **Automated alerts** — notifications trigger for goals approaching target dates without progress updates

### 4.3 Goal Sharing and Cross-Team Alignment

Where the client's operating model requires cross-functional goal alignment, APPSolve configures shared goal functionality — enabling a goal owned by one team to be visible to and tracked by related teams — supporting matrix-structured organisations and project-based environments.

### 4.4 Goal and Performance Integration

APPSolve configures the integration between Goal Management and Performance Management so that goal plan completion and achievement ratings automatically populate the performance review form. This eliminates manual re-entry and ensures that the performance rating reflects actual goal outcomes — not a separate, parallel assessment.

## Section 5 — Performance Management

### 5.1 Performance Review Cycle Configuration

APPSolve implements Oracle Fusion Performance Management to structure and automate the client's performance review process. The review cycle configuration is one of the most closely customised aspects of any HCM implementation — APPSolve dedicates a full workshop session to mapping the client's existing performance process before designing the Oracle configuration.

**Review cycle elements APPSolve configures:**

| Element | Configuration |
|---|---|
| Review cycle types | Annual, mid-year, quarterly, probation, and ad hoc cycles — each independently configured |
| Performance document | The review form template — sections, questions, rating scales, goal plan sections, narrative fields |
| Rating scales | Configurable 3-, 4-, or 5-point scales with descriptors per rating level |
| Participant roles | Employee self-assessment, manager assessment, matrix manager, HR, and skip-level sections |
| Workflow | The sequence of steps — self-assessment → manager assessment → calibration → approval → acknowledgement |
| Section weighting | Relative weighting of competency, goal, and overall rating sections |
| Timeline | Configurable start dates, step deadlines, and escalation rules |

### 5.2 Competency Assessment

APPSolve integrates the client's competency framework into the performance review form so that the review captures both goal achievement and behavioural demonstration:

- Competencies drawn directly from the job profile for the worker's current role
- Rating of each competency against the configured scale
- Narrative evidence fields for each competency rating
- Competency ratings feed the overall performance score according to the configured weighting

### 5.3 360-Degree Feedback

360-degree feedback is a standard Oracle Fusion Performance Management capability that APPSolve configures as part of the Talent Management engagement. APPSolve configures Oracle Fusion's participant feedback functionality to solicit structured input from peers, direct reports, and stakeholders — providing a multi-source view of each worker's performance and behaviour that enriches the manager's assessment.

**Standard 360 configuration APPSolve delivers:**

- **Participant selection** — the feedback requester (employee or manager) nominates raters; HR or the manager approves the participant list
- **Questionnaire design** — competency-based questionnaire aligned to the client's performance model; APPSolve configures the questions and rating scales in the Performance Design Workshop
- **Attribution model** — feedback may be configured as attributed (rater name visible) or anonymous (aggregated), depending on the client's governance preference
- **Aggregated results** — 360 feedback responses are presented to the manager and employee in aggregated form within the performance review form
- **Integration with review** — the 360 result feeds directly into the performance document alongside the manager assessment and self-assessment

**Configuration decisions confirmed in the Performance Design Workshop:** number of rater types; minimum and maximum participants per rater category; attribution vs anonymity; questionnaire structure; feedback window timing within the review cycle.

*Note: Advanced customer-specific workflow extensions, bespoke review processes, or non-standard 360 architectures that cannot be accommodated within Oracle standard functionality may require separate scoping.*

### 5.4 Performance Calibration

APPSolve configures Oracle Fusion's talent calibration functionality to support the performance calibration process:

- **Calibration dashboards** — HR and talent leaders see the full population on a configurable matrix (typically performance vs potential — the 9-box)
- **Live adjustments** — managers and HR can adjust ratings during the calibration session from within the Oracle UI, with changes reflected in the worker's performance record
- **Distribution visibility** — rating distribution by department, grade, or business unit is displayed in real time, supporting normative calibration
- **Audit trail** — all calibration adjustments are recorded with the reviewer identity and timestamp

### 5.5 Performance and Compensation Integration

Where Oracle Fusion Workforce Compensation (W3S1-005) is in scope, APPSolve configures the integration between Performance Management and Compensation so that the performance rating automatically populates the compensation planning worksheet. This enables compensation managers to allocate salary increases, bonuses, and equity awards within approved budgets — informed by performance outcome.

## Section 6 — Talent Review and Succession Planning

### 6.1 Talent Review

APPSolve implements Oracle Fusion Talent Review to give HR leaders and senior managers a structured, data-driven view of the talent population — enabling informed decisions about development, deployment, retention, and succession.

**Talent review configuration APPSolve delivers:**

| Component | Description |
|---|---|
| 9-box matrix | Performance vs potential matrix — APPSolve configures the axes, thresholds, and quadrant labels to the client's model |
| Population scope | Configurable scope — the review may cover the full organisation, a specific business unit, a grade band, or a leadership population |
| Talent indicators | Risk of loss, impact of loss, mobility preference, promotability rating — all configured and visible on the talent card |
| Talent notes | Private HR and manager notes attached to each worker's talent record, visible to authorised reviewers |
| Action plans | Post-review action items linked to the worker record — feeding development plans and succession updates |

### 6.2 Succession Planning

APPSolve implements Oracle Fusion Succession Planning to enable the client to identify successors for key roles and manage the readiness and development of the succession pipeline.

**Succession planning capability APPSolve configures:**

| Capability | Description |
|---|---|
| Critical role identification | Roles designated as critical are flagged; succession plan depth is measured against each |
| Succession candidate pools | Nominated successors ranked by readiness level (Ready Now, Ready 1–2 Years, Ready 3+ Years) |
| Candidate profile visibility | The succession planner sees the nominee's talent card, performance history, and development plan in one view |
| Bench strength reporting | Dashboards show bench depth per critical role, by business unit, and across the organisation |
| Succession gap alerts | Roles with insufficient succession depth are highlighted for HR action |
| Incumbent comparison | Candidate profiles can be compared directly against the incumbent's and the job profile's requirements |

### 6.3 Succession and Talent Review Integration

APPSolve configures the Talent Review and Succession Planning modules as an integrated workflow: talent review assessments (performance, potential, risk, mobility) directly inform succession candidacy decisions. A worker assessed as high-potential and ready for advancement in the talent review is surfaced automatically as a succession candidate for relevant roles, reducing administrative effort and ensuring that succession decisions are grounded in current, validated talent data.

APPSolve implements Oracle Fusion Career Development to support employees in understanding their career options, planning their development, and taking ownership of their growth within the organisation. Career Development integrates with Talent Profiles, Goal Management, and Learning Cloud (where in scope) to create a connected talent development experience.

### 7.2 Development Plans

APPSolve configures Oracle Fusion development plans to connect the skills gap identified in the talent profile to structured development actions:

- **Development goals** — linked to the employee's talent profile gaps and career pathway requirements
- **Learning activities** — formal training, on-the-job development, mentoring, and stretch assignments recorded as development actions
- **Learning Cloud integration** — where Oracle Learning Cloud is in scope, development plan actions link directly to learning programmes (see W3S1-004)
- **Manager collaboration** — development plans are co-owned by the employee and manager; both can add, edit, and review development actions

### 7.3 Internal Opportunity Marketplace

## Section 11 — Implementation Approach

### 11.1 Talent Phase Sequencing

APPSolve delivers Talent Management in the second phase of the Oracle HCM engagement, after HCM Core is live and stable. The Talent phase follows the same OUM-structured delivery sequence as HCM Core:

| Step | Talent Management activity |
|---|---|
| **Talent Design Workshop** | Profile design; performance document design; goal framework design; succession model design |
| **Configuration Decision Log** | All Talent design decisions documented and signed off before build |
| **Talent Build (CRP 1)** | Full Talent configuration delivered; HR team walks through the configured talent process end-to-end |
| **Talent CRP 2** | Corrections from CRP 1 implemented; second walkthrough with stakeholders |
| **Integration Testing** | Performance-to-compensation link; talent-to-learning link; OTBI reports |
| **Talent UAT** | Client HR and talent team test full talent cycle: goal-setting → mid-year → year-end → calibration → succession update |
| **Talent Go-Live** | Talent modules activated in production; full performance cycle launched |
| **Hypercare** | Full APPSolve team support through the first complete performance cycle |

### 11.2 Performance Cycle Timing

APPSolve plans the Talent Management go-live around the client's performance calendar. If the client's annual review cycle begins in Q1, the Talent go-live must be completed before the cycle opens. APPSolve confirms the performance calendar in the Talent Design Workshop and back-plans the project timeline accordingly.

**Rule:** Do not go live with Performance Management if the configured review cycle cannot complete at least one full cycle before the next configuration change is required. APPSolve confirms cycle timing with the client before any configuration is committed.

### 11.3 Change Management for Talent Adoption

Talent Management represents a significant change to the employee and manager experience — more so than most other HCM modules. APPSolve's Talent change management approach:

- **Manager training** — dedicated manager sessions on goal setting, performance assessment, and the calibration process
- **Employee communication** — structured communication plan for goal-setting launch and performance cycle opening
- **Train-the-trainer** — HR super-users are trained to support managers and employees through the first cycle
- **Quick reference guides** — APPSolve produces one-page guides for the most common employee and manager actions in each module

### 11.4 Testing

**CRP focus for Talent Management:**
APPSolve tests the end-to-end talent cycle in CRP — from profile creation through goal-setting, mid-year check-in, year-end assessment, calibration, succession nomination, and development plan creation. The CRP test script is designed to walk through a complete talent lifecycle, not individual module screens.

**UAT for Talent Management:**
APPSolve provides role-based UAT scripts for employee, manager, HR administrator, talent reviewer, and succession planner roles. UAT sign-off requires completion of a full talent cycle by client testers in the UAT environment.
